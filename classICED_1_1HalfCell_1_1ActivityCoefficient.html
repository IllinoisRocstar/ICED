void SolvePhysics ( )

Solver for Activity Coefficient.

The activity coefficient is a dimensionless quantity, dependent on temperature, pressure, and solution composition, that scales the chemical potential to account for solution non-ideality. The ActivityCoefficient SolvePhysics function is a submodule that solves for the mean molar activity coefficient of a binary electrolyte based on the concentration of the electrolyte . The current implementation assumes a 4th-order polynomial for the activity coefficient, but other functions could be added. The coefficients of the polynomial are read from the input file(s).

where and are constants. The constants are expressed in the function below in the vector pActivityCoeff starting with the 0th index.

The coefficient is used when dealing with the electrolytic current used in modules such as OhmsLawElectrolyte.
